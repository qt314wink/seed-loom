# Epistemic Design Engine — Paradox Preservation

Status: proposed v0.1

## Purpose

Paradox preservation prevents the engine from flattening a valuable tension merely because a single answer is easier to store or execute. A paradox is not any disagreement. It is a pair or set of claims, forces, values, or requirements that appear incompatible yet may both be necessary to the identity, function, ethics, or future adaptability of the system.

Examples include handcrafted yet computational, authoritative yet warm, stable yet evolving, explicit yet mysterious, and efficient yet exploratory.

## Paradox record

Every paradox records:

- participating claims, forces, or requirements;
- evidence supporting each side;
- whether the conflict is logical, practical, temporal, contextual, semantic, or aesthetic;
- identity value of preserving it;
- cost and risk of preserving it;
- cost and risk of resolving it;
- missing information;
- containment strategy;
- review trigger;
- current state: candidate, confirmed, preserved, resolved, dissolved, or superseded.

## Recognition logic

A tension becomes a paradox candidate when:

- satisfying one requirement measurably weakens another;
- both sides have independent support;
- removing either side causes identity loss or stakeholder harm;
- different contexts legitimately require different expressions;
- evidence is insufficient to choose without false certainty;
- the tension generates useful exploration rather than simple confusion.

It is not preserved when one side is unsupported, unsafe, incoherent, or merely a duplicate phrased as opposition.

## Preservation strategies

- **Temporal alternation**: different qualities dominate at different stages.
- **Spatial partition**: different surfaces, modules, or contexts express different sides.
- **Layering**: one side provides structure while another supplies surface, behavior, or narrative.
- **Role separation**: different actors or subsystems hold different obligations.
- **Range maintenance**: the system operates within a bounded tension rather than one point.
- **Explicit deferral**: no resolution until named evidence arrives.
- **Dual representation**: both interpretations remain visible and queryable.

Preservation is an engineered condition, not indecision. It must define how work proceeds while the tension remains alive.

## Constraints

- Safety, legality, consent, and accessibility cannot be weakened merely to preserve aesthetic tension.
- A paradox cannot be declared to avoid making an ordinary reversible decision.
- Preserved tensions require an owner, containment mechanism, and revisit trigger.
- Contradictory implementation requirements must be partitioned or tested; they cannot coexist as impossible instructions.
- Confidence must be assigned independently to each side.
- Resolution may not erase the historical paradox record.

## Gates

G0 legitimacy: both sides have evidence or explicit stakeholder authority.  
G1 identity test: document what is lost by deleting either side.  
G2 action continuity: the project can still take a next step.  
G3 containment: conflict cannot leak into undefined behavior or unsafe output.  
G4 observability: users and reviewers can see where the tension is expressed.  
G5 revisitability: conditions for resolution, dissolution, or continuation are explicit.

## Benchmarks

- 100% of preserved paradoxes include evidence for each side.
- 100% include a containment strategy and revisit trigger.
- Zero preserved paradoxes produce hard-constraint violations.
- At least 80% reviewer agreement that deleting either side would materially reduce identity or fit.
- Decision latency introduced by preservation remains within the declared project tolerance.
- At least one executable artifact or experiment demonstrates the tension rather than merely describing it.

## QA

Run false-paradox detection, unsupported-side checks, safety override checks, implementation contradiction tests, and reviewer comparison between preserved and prematurely resolved variants. Validate that preservation increases meaningful distinctiveness, adaptability, or stakeholder fit rather than visual or conceptual clutter.

## Conditions for continued life

A paradox remains live only while both sides retain support and the containment strategy works. It must be resolved, dissolved, or superseded when evidence decisively weakens one side, context changes remove the tension, the cost exceeds declared value, or preservation prevents required action. Review must occur at every major release or when its trigger fires.
